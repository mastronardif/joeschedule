Objective/Treatment Areas Addressed:
Developmental Level/ Focus Area Goal +/- Comments
1. Regulation John will increase ability to maintain self-regulation (calm and organized state/remain calm and organized while participating in a pleasurable activity alone or with someone else) for 15 minutes. Currently he is able to remain self-regulated for 5 minutes.
Objective 1a John will remain regulated in a variety of arousal states as needed to participate in an activity that has a beginning middle and end for 10 minutes +
John will return to a calm state with the support of another person using co-regulation strategies when he is distressed, within 5 min of a triggering experience. Currently he needs max support with inconsistent results to return to a calm state once he is distressed.
Objective 1b: ohn will have a positive response when another person meets him just below his arousal level (when distressed) and will grade their interaction utilizing graded coping strategies (paced tone and position in space, playing external music (harp-hush little baby), spray bottle (cold water), having him hum/sing, rocking on swing/chair) within 5 min after becoming distressed. +
2. Engagement John will sustain engagement (without fragmenting) with an adult in a non-demanding, non-over stimulating unstructured/play interaction/activity for 15 min out of 1 hr (e.g., child is able to participate in a simple back and forth interaction, game or activity without being distracted. He is currently able to do this less than 5 min in 1 hr. +
Objective 2a John will initiate passing toys or objects pertaining to an activity to another person with indirect or gestural cueing 5 times within the same activity
John will improve his ability to sustain engagement through a range of emotions including excitement, disorganization, sadness, frustration and anger 3 out of 5 times during self-care tasks or daily routines (e.g., remains connected to interaction). He is currently seldom able to do this. Less than 1 time out of 5 challenging situations) +
Objective 2b: John will join in a cooperative self-care task with indirect or gestural cueing (i.e. putting on shoes/skates, washing hands, eating) 1 step out of a multi-step task
John will maintain 5+ physical circle(s) of interaction with a familiar adult in non-demanding, non-over stimulating play (e.g. will pass a ball back and forth with an adult) He is currently able to maintain 2 circles of interactions with moderate cues. +
Objective 2c: John will mirror an action of a familiar adult (i.e. putting dominos in a row, gathering materials) in the absence of direct verbal prompts for turn-taking 2 out of 3 attempts
3. Intentionality John will increase spontaneous initiations for interactions with a familiar adult to expand exploration 3 out of 5 times during play (e.g., reach to give or get an object from familiar adult). He is currently able to do this 1 out of 5 times.
Objective 3a John will choose an aspect of an activity that fits within his desired mode of exploration in a supportive play interaction add 3+ sequences/steps to activity (i.e. rather than picking up and pouring beans, picking up one bean at a time or pouring from one container to another)
John will increase overall intentionality using positive language, or gestures to express oneself during new routines or activities (e.g., requests, protests, express an idea etc. vs. acting out) 10 times during a 1 hr. therapy session. He is currently able to do this with familiar needs only, Bathroom, eating, drinking which occurs ~2 times per hours.
Objective 3b: John will chose 4-5 activities to build a choice board from a field of 10 icons (9 available activities plus an icon representing “something else” that isn’t on the board)
John will be able to initiate and follow through 3 out of 5 steps of a self-care task such as dressing, brushing teeth, and/or toileting without the support of another person. He is currently able to initiate the initial step of familiar and routine self-care task < 50% of the time.
Objective 3c: John will follow first 1-2 steps of a self-care task utilizing a visual schedule/sequence board (i.e. step 1: flush toilet, step 2: wash hands, step 3: dry hands)
4. Problem Solving / Motor Planning John will demonstrate improved motor planning abilities using 1 of 3 new techniques (requesting specific assistance, persisting, and/or adapting his actions) as needed to overcome challenges with routine tasks (such as when getting ready to go for a walk), with no more than 2 verbal cues and needed gestures to prompt him to use the strategies when he faces a challenge. He is currently able to do this only with full caregiver support and or max prompts.
Objective 4a: John will retrieve necessary clothing items (socks, shoes, or jacket) when prompted by an adult that we will be going outside with minimal assistance -
Objective 4b: John will participate in the planning of an activity (i.e. gather necessary items, choose a location, etc.) with less than 2 indirect and gestural cues per 1 activity
